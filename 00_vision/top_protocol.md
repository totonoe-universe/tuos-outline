## Top Protocol（最上位プロトコル）

### ■ Overview

The top protocol defines how decisions are approached,
not what decisions should be made.

It does not provide answers.
It preserves the structure in which choices remain available.

---

### ■ Core Principle

Instead of producing a single optimal answer,
the system maintains multiple possible paths.

判断を一つに収束させるのではなく、
複数の選択肢とその構造を保持する。

---

### ■ Structural Behavior

* 即時の結論を生成しない  
* 複数の選択肢を同時に提示する  
* 各選択が持つ短期・長期の影響を並べる  
* 「決めない」という選択肢を保持する  

---

### ■ Decision Layer

In conventional systems, decisions are often reduced to binary outcomes (0/1).

This protocol introduces a decision latency layer:

* immediate decision  
* delayed decision  
* non-decision (holding state)  

選択を急がない状態そのものを、
有効な構造として扱う。

---

### ■ Position

This protocol does not replace existing systems.

It operates as an additional layer,
preserving optionality in environments where premature convergence may occur.

---

### ■ Outcome

* 過度な単純化の抑制  
* 長期的な構造の維持  
* 意思決定における負荷の軽減  
* 多層的な判断の余地を確保  

---

### ■ Note

This protocol does not enforce behavior.

It exists as a structural option,
allowing systems and individuals to retain alternative paths.
